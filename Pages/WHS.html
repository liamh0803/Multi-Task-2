While Shooting Films Risks

Firstly when filming a movie in a public space many risks reveal themselves, these risks include things such as tripping over objects which can be caused by wires, props or other things that could trip people over.

Secondly lifting hazards, filming equipment can be heavy when transporting it therefore risk lessening procedures should be used to reduce risk of injury such as lifting with your legs instead of your back which will reduce strain on your back. 


Finally eing under pressure due to time restraints will make people rush and therefore not be as alert therefore making them miss risks in the environment and therefore increasing chances of injury.

When Editing

When Editing risks are minimal they still exist risks still exist, the largest of which is the ergonomics of where the editing is taking place, Bad ergonomics can lead to back, neck problems, low circulation and psychological issues  due to people being hunched over at their desks for long periods of timeand eye levels in comparison to their screen making them need to bend their necks.

When Running an event

When running a public film event safety measures need to be put in place to protect people if things go wrong, Short film festivals include many people and take place in venues so therefore evacuation procedures need to be put in place such as an evacuation diagram and an exit. (Shown To Right) These need to be put in place in case of a fire or emergency so everyone can get away from danger safely, another risk during these festivals is theft due to there potentially being expensive equipment which could be stolen easily. Another example of a danger in a public event is sudden accidents, these can include injuries, collapsing, tripping on equipment and so on.

Ways To Minimize Risk
Many Ways to Minimize risk when filming editing and running an event exist such as Film Insurance when you are filming, when editing you can buy an ergonomic desk such as a sit and stand desk, a chair, a monitor where the top is level with your eyes or a monitor stand, having your arms angled to be perpendicular to the desk and reduce repetitive movements which helps reduce fatigue when editing. Due to these it is important to have a first aid kit to tend to any injuries that may occur during the event.
